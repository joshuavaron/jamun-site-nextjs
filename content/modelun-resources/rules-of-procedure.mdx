## Article I: General Rules

### 1.1 Scope

These rules apply to all JAMUN committees unless otherwise specified by the Secretariat.

### 1.2 Purpose

These Rules of Procedure are designed to facilitate orderly and productive debate, enabling delegates to engage in diplomacy, negotiation, and collaborative problem-solving in the spirit of the United Nations.

### 1.3 Authority of the Chair

The Chair shall maintain order, rule on points and motions, and exercise discretion on all procedural matters. The Chair's rulings are final unless overturned by a two-thirds majority vote.

### 1.4 Secretariat Interpretation

The Secretariat holds final authority to interpret these rules. Any disputes regarding rule interpretation shall be referred to the Secretariat, whose decisions are binding.

### 1.5 Amendments to Rules

These rules may be suspended or amended during a session by a two-thirds majority vote, subject to Secretariat approval.

## Article II: Debate Format

### 2.1 Speakers' List

a. The Chair maintains the speakers' list throughout debate.

b. Delegates wishing to speak shall raise their placards.

c. A delegate may speak only when recognized by the Chair.

d. Default speaking time is **90 seconds** unless modified.

e. Committee may not proceed if the speakers' list is empty.

### 2.2 Moderated Caucus

a. Requires a motion specifying total time, individual speaking time, and topic.

b. Requires a simple majority to pass.

c. Maximum individual speaking time is **one minute**.

d. The Chair calls on delegates who raise their placards.

e. The moving delegate may request the first or last speaking position.

f. Delegates may speak multiple times if time permits.

### 2.3 Unmoderated Caucus

a. Requires a motion specifying total time.

b. Requires a simple majority to pass.

c. Maximum duration is **20 minutes**.

d. Delegates may move freely and negotiate informally.

### 2.4 Yielding Time

a. A delegate may yield remaining speaking time to the Chair or to another delegate.

b. Time yielded to the Chair concludes the speaker's remarks.

c. Time yielded to another delegate may not be yielded again.

## Article III: Points and Motions

### 3.1 Points (In Order of Precedence)

a. **Point of Order:** Addresses procedural violations. May be raised at any time.

b. **Point of Inquiry:** Questions about procedure. May be raised when there is no active speaker.

c. **Point of Personal Privilege:** Personal comfort concerns. May be raised when there is no active speaker.

d. **Point of Information:** A question directed to the current speaker. May only be raised during a speech with the consent of the Chair.

### 3.2 Motions (In Order of Precedence)

a. Motion to Appeal the Chair: Challenges a ruling by the Chair. This motion cannot be ruled dilatory and requires a two-thirds majority to pass.

b. Motion to Adjourn: Ends the conference session

c. Motion to Suspend: Suspends for recess

d. Motion to Enter Voting Procedure: Moves the committee into voting on draft resolutions

e. Motion to Close Debate: Ends debate on topic

f. Motion to Open Debate: Opens debate on a new topic

g. Motion to Open the Speakers' List: Opens or reopens the speakers' list

h. Motion for Unmoderated Caucus

i. Motion for Moderated Caucus

### 3.3 Dilatory Motions

The Chair may rule any motion dilatory if it is deemed to be obstructing the progress of debate. A motion ruled dilatory will not be entertained.

### 3.4 Order of Consideration

Motions shall be considered in order from most to least disruptive. Disruption is determined first by precedence (as listed above) and then by time (longer durations are more disruptive).

## Article IV: Working Papers and Resolutions

### 4.1 Working Papers

a. Working papers are informal documents used to develop ideas.

b. Working papers do not require sponsors or signatories.

c. Introduction of working papers is at the discretion of the Chair.

### 4.2 Draft Resolutions

a. Draft resolutions require sponsors (authors) and signatories (supporters).

b. The maximum number of sponsors is **5% of delegates in committee, rounded up**.

c. The minimum number of signatories is **20% of present delegates, rounded down**.

d. Draft resolutions must follow proper format, including preambulatory and operative clauses.

### 4.3 Amendments

a. **Friendly amendments** are approved by all sponsors and are automatically incorporated into the draft resolution.

b. **Unfriendly amendments** require a vote to be incorporated and must have their own sponsors and signatories.

## Article V: Voting Procedure

### 5.1 Procedural Votes

a. All delegates must vote on procedural matters; abstentions are not permitted.

b. A simple majority is required for a procedural vote to pass.

c. Procedural votes are conducted by a show of placards.

### 5.2 Substantive Votes

a. Delegates who responded "Present and Voting" during roll call may not abstain on substantive votes.

b. Delegates who responded "Present" during roll call may abstain on substantive votes.

c. A simple majority of non-abstaining votes is required for a substantive vote to pass.

### 5.3 Roll Call Vote

a. Any delegate may request a roll call vote before voting begins.

b. During a roll call vote, delegates may vote "Yes," "No," "Abstain," or "Pass."

c. A delegate who votes "Pass" will have one opportunity to cast their vote at the end of the roll call.

## Article VI: Conduct

### 6.1 Decorum

a. Delegates shall maintain diplomatic courtesy at all times.

b. Personal attacks are prohibited.

c. Use of electronic devices is at the discretion of the Chair.

### 6.2 Formal Attire

a. Delegates are expected to wear formal attire during committee sessions.

b. JAMUN staff understand that formal attire means a variety of things in different cultures and for students from different backgrounds.

c. Acceptable attire includes suits, dresses, slacks, blouses, and blazers.

d. Delegates are politely asked not to wear clothing with distracting logos, messages, or images.

### 6.3 Violations

a. For a first offense, the delegate will receive a warning from the Chair.

b. For a second offense, a note will be sent to the delegate's faculty advisor.

c. For a third offense, the delegate will be removed from committee.
