## 18 HPC § 101. Culpability Defined

A person cannot be guilty of an offense unless they acted purposefully, knowingly, recklessly, or negligently — whichever mental state the law requires — as to each element of that offense.

## 18 HPC § 102. Purposefully Defined

A person acts purposefully when it is their conscious objective — that is, what they are actually trying to do — to act in a certain way or to bring about a certain result.

## 18 HPC § 103. Knowingly Defined

A person acts knowingly when they are aware of what they are doing, or are practically certain that their conduct will cause a particular result — even if causing that result was not their goal.

## 18 HPC § 104. Recklessly Defined

A person acts recklessly when they are aware of a substantial and unjustifiable risk that a result will follow from their conduct and consciously choose to ignore it. Ignoring the risk must be a gross deviation from how a law-abiding person would act in the same situation.

## 18 HPC § 105. Negligently Defined

A person acts negligently when they should be aware of a substantial and unjustifiable risk that a result will follow from their conduct, but fail to notice it. That failure must be a gross deviation from the care a reasonable person would use in the same situation.

## 18 HPC § 201. Causation

A defendant's conduct is the legal cause of a result when two things are true: the result would not have happened but for the defendant's conduct, and the result was a natural and foreseeable consequence of that conduct.

## 18 HPC § 301. Criminal Homicide Defined

A person commits criminal homicide if they purposefully, knowingly, recklessly, or negligently cause the death of another human being. Criminal homicide is either murder in the first degree or murder in the second degree.

## 18 HPC § 303. Murder

**(A)** Criminal homicide constitutes **murder in the first degree** when it is committed purposefully or knowingly.

**(B)** Criminal homicide constitutes **murder in the second degree** when it is committed recklessly or negligently.
