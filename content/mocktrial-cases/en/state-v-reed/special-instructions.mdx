_Revised May 9, 2025_

These special instructions govern the trial of this case. They control over any contrary inference a team might draw from the case documents. Where these instructions refer to the "Rules," they mean the JAMUN Mock Trial Rules; where they refer to the "Rules of Evidence," they mean the Highfield Rules of Evidence.

1. **Witness Selection and Captains' Meeting.** The Captains' Meeting Form governs witness selection, character genders, and each character's personal traits. Any substitute exhibit or demonstrative permitted by these instructions must be disclosed to the opposing team at the Captains' Meeting.

2. **Revision Dates.** A revision date indicates only that participants are using the most recent version of a document at trial. It says nothing about the history of the case, and no party may use a revision date to introduce or argue any fact.

3. **Documents Are What They Purport to Be.** A witness must acknowledge authorship of any document that purports to be authored by them, and the authenticity of any signature that purports to be theirs. A witness whom the materials describe as familiar with a document must acknowledge that they are familiar with it and that it is the same version as the one in this packet.

4. **Party Representatives.** Peyton Reed and Ryan Cole are the only permissible party representatives under Rule 615 of the Highfield Rules of Evidence.

5. **Closed-Universe Problem.** The only legal authority a competitor may cite, or a judge may rely on, for any purpose is the material in "Relevant Highfield Law" and the Highfield Rules of Evidence. All participants must acknowledge this if asked.

6. **Fifth Amendment.** No witness may refuse to answer a question — and no attorney may instruct a witness not to answer — on the basis of the privilege against self-incrimination.

7. **Black-and-White Copies.** No objection may be raised on the ground that a document, exhibit, or demonstrative was reproduced in black-and-white.

8. **Charges and Defenses.** The prosecution must pursue Count One — murder in the first degree. The defense may argue that Peyton Reed is guilty only of the lesser included offense of murder in the second degree, or that Reed is not guilty of either charge. The State of Highfield bears the burden of proof throughout; even if the defense concedes the lesser offense, the State must still prove its case, and the defense may not argue that an element of the offense is irrelevant on that basis.

9. **Best Evidence Limited to the Packet.** No attorney may object under Rule 1002 of the Highfield Rules of Evidence when the original writing, recording, or photograph in question is not among the documents in this packet. Because no Apex video footage exists in this packet, no team may raise any objection under Article X of the Rules of Evidence concerning Apex videos, livestreams, or clips.

10. **Online Metrics and Comments.** No team may invent subscriber counts, view counts, revenue or payout figures, comments, or other Apex analytics beyond those stated in the case documents.

11. **Characteristics as Material Inventions.** Teams may use distinctive speech or mannerisms to develop a character, but may not use them to invent material facts or to contradict any stipulation — for example, a team may not argue from a student's speech pattern that a witness was incapable of making a statement attributed to them in the materials. No competitor may suggest, in examination or argument, that the student playing Peyton Reed lacked the physical or mental capacity to commit the alleged acts. Violation may constitute an improper invention of material fact and is subject to review by the Competition Integrity Committee.

12. **Reading Documents Onto the Record.** Time spent reading any part of a document onto the record is deducted from the reading team's direct- or cross-examination time, depending on whose case-in-chief it occurs during. Reading onto the record may not occur before opening statements or after the defense closes its case-in-chief.

13. **Defendant at Counsel Table.** Because identifying the defendant is an element of the State's case, the defense must either seat a student as Peyton Reed at counsel table or announce during pretrial that Reed is constructively present at counsel table, including a description of Reed.

14. **Photo of West Bailey.** The prosecution may elect to supply a photograph of West Bailey. The photo must show one person from the shoulders up, on a neutral-colored background, with no material inventions, and must be shown to the defense at the Captains' Meeting. Violation may constitute an improper invention of material fact and is subject to review by the Competition Integrity Committee.

15. **No Guilty Portrayals.** No testifying witness other than Peyton Reed may portray, explicitly or implicitly, that they are the person who caused West Bailey's death.
