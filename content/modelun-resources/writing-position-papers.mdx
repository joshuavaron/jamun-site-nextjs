## What is a Position Paper?

A position paper is a one-page document that outlines your country's position on the topics being discussed in your committee. It demonstrates your preparation and helps chairs understand your country's stance.

### Structure of a Position Paper

#### Header
Include your country name, committee name, and the topic.

#### Paragraph 1: Background
- Brief overview of the topic
- Why this issue matters globally
- Historical context if relevant

#### Paragraph 2: Your Country's Position
- How does this topic affect your country?
- What is your country's official stance?
- What actions has your country taken?

#### Paragraph 3: Proposed Solutions
- What does your country want to see happen?
- What specific actions should the committee take?
- What would your country support in a resolution?

### Tips for Success

1. **Be concise**: Keep it to one page
2. **Stay in character**: Write from your country's perspective
3. **Be specific**: Include concrete policy proposals
4. **Use formal language**: Maintain diplomatic tone
5. **Cite sources**: Reference official UN documents when possible
